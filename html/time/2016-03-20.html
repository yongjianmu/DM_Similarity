Anti-Trump Protester Punched and Kicked at Rally

Another Donald Trump rally was plagued by violence when a protester was punched in the face and kicked while being escorted out the Republican front-runner’s event in Arizona. The candidate has denied that there's been any violence at his events

Paris Terror Suspect Planned New Attacks From Brussels

Terror suspect Salah Abdeslam has told investigators he was planning to "restart something" from Brussels. Authorities are taking the claim seriously because they've found heavy weapons and a new network of people around him in Belgium

Zootopia Tramples Allegiant at Box Office

Zootopia continues to run wild over its rivals in its third weekend in theaters, stomping all over the launch of "Allegiant," the third film in the Divergent series. The animated Disney film took in $38 million to take first place and pass the $200 million mark domestically

Senate Majority Leader Rules Out 'Lame Duck' Confirmation

Senate Majority Leader Mitch McConnell tried to shut down Beltway musings that President Obama's latest pick for the Supreme Court could still find a way to be confirmed in the lame duck session if Hillary Clinton wins the election

Bus Crash Kills 13 Students in Spain

Thirteen people died and 30 others were injured Sunday after a bus carrying foreign exchange university students crashed in Spain. The bus was carrying more than 50 passengers when it appeared to have hit barriers on the right-hand side of the highway

Sherwin-Williams Will Buy Valspar for $11.3 Billion

Paint company Sherwin-Williams is buying rival Valspar for $11.3 billion in a move that it says will expand its reach in Asia and Europe. Sherwin-Williams will pay $113 per share in cash

Gawker to Appeal Verdict Awarding Hulk Hogan $115M

Gawker will appeal the verdict reached in favor of Hulk Hogan in the lawsuit he brought over the company's publication of a sex tape featuring the professional wrestler. The jury awarded Hogan $115 in compensatory damages in addition to punitive damages

Flight Recorders Were Badly Damaged in Russia Plane Crash

Flight recorders from the Flydubai passenger jet that crashed in southern Russia, killing all 62 people on board, are “significantly damaged,” authorities said, but fficials were working to recover the information from the damaged recorders

5 Dead in Turkey Suicide Attack

A suicide attacker detonated a bomb on Istanbul's main pedestrian shopping street, killing five people, the city's governor said. Turkey's health minister said 36 people were wounded in the attack including 12 foreign nationals

Michigan State Loss Means Your Bracket Is Busted

In the latest bracket-busting move, No. 15 seed Middle Tennessee Blue Raiders beat the No. 2 seed Michigan State Spartans, a team many predicted would win it all this year. The 90-81 loss was a shock to MSU fans and haters alike

Tyler Perry Talks 'The Passion' and the Election

Actor and director Tyler Perry spoke to TIME ahead of his live musical special The Passion, which follows the last days of Jesus' life on earth. He also talked about the controversy over the Oscars, and whether he'll endorse a presidential candidate
